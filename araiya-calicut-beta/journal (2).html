The Journal

Calicut - Your Cocoon of Calm

Imagine… a river as it flows gently towards the sea, shimmering in the sunshine. Coconut palms dot the banks and sway gently in the breeze. The air is fragrant with the sweet scent of frangipani and jasmine. Bougainvilla and Hibiscus create brilliant flashes of colour against the emerald foliage.Tranquil. Tropical. That’s just one of the reasons why we call Kerala “God’s Own Country”.

Calicut lies on the Kerala coast, where the Kadalundi river meets the Arabian Sea – and is one of South India’s best kept secrets. If you’re wondering why you should go to Calicut – ask us! At the Araiya Calicut, we can give you several excellent reasons to visit Calicut.

Calicut has always been a bustling port, and a melting pot for a variety of different cultures, flavours, folk and influences that have washed up on her shores. A stroll through the historic center of the city reveals quaint old-world architecture and snippets of untold history and folklore.

The piquant aroma of delicious North Kerala home-cooking draws our attention to the fact that this is a great place for foodies – a culinary destination of tremendous lineage! Elegant women draped in beautiful handwoven saris remind us that this is also a city with a rich textile heritage – after all, Calico originated here and was shipped out in large bolts from Calicut’s port to far-flung shores. A short drive up the coast will bring you to the Tasara weaving works, where artists from around the world collaborate with local handloom weavers to create eye-catching patterns and designs.

Terracotta handicrafts and tiles have been an industry in Kerala for centuries. When missionaries from Basel in Switzerland found themselves on the Malabar coast, they were so intrigued by terracotta, that they refined the craft and left behind a rich legacy of terracotta craftsmanship that endures even today. South India is also famous for it’s woodwork, and you can visit the workshops of several artisans who create exquisite carvings out of locally sourced wood. Speaking of arts and crafts, don’t miss a visit to the Lalithkala Academy to learn a little more about Malabar’s artistic heritage. It is a stunning gallery where talented local and tribal artists display their work.
Movement is magic! On the Malabar coast, a rich culture of performing arts and martial arts, makes this an absolute truth. Several centres groom young artistes into world class performers in dance, drama and the martial arts. From Kathakali to Theyyam to Kalaripayattu, these traditions are alive and well in Calicut!

One of Calicut’s greatest legacies is the Kottakkal Arya Vaidya Sala – a legendary center for Ayurvedic science and practice. At Araiya Calicut, our Ayurvedic practitioners are specially trained and groomed at the Kottakal Arya Vaidya Sala, to bring the miracle of Ayurvedic wellbeing to you in it’s most authentic form.

Is your curiosity piqued enough? Visit our resort in Kerala and discover one of the best kept travel secrets in South India. The Araiya Calicut – your cocoon of calm on the Kerala coast.
